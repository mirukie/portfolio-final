About Me.

Hello! I'm Jocelyn Le, a second-year Computational Media student at the Georgia Institute of Technology.

My fascination with both the technical and aesthetic sides of digital art is what led me to pursue this major. I have always been in touch with my creative side, and I'm looking to expand my artistry into the field of technology. I am an avid Adobe Photoshop user, and I am currently studying programming languages such as Java, CSS, and HTML to further my technical skills.

I am also serving as a proud member of Georgia Tech's Asian American Student Association. As a part of the Junior Design Board, I create designs for campus-wide events and help promote inclusivity amongst our Asian community through other creative events.

Projects.

Some of my bigger works from Fall 2022.
You can click on the A-Frame and Buzz's Bakery icons to explore these projects further!

3D Modeling

AFrame Introduction

In a team of three, we learned how to use a 3D modeling program by recreating a piece of art (Composition 2 in Red Blue and Yellow by Mondrian). We expanded the vision by creating it in a museum-like space with an ethereal background.

T-Shirt Design

GT Tour of Asia 2022

This was the t-shirt design that I created for Tour of Asia, a Georgia Tech signature event hosted by the Asian American Student Association. The event was attended by over 1300 people and about 20 other campus organizations. It also became a sticker and tote bag design.

HTML-Based Game

Buzz's Bakery

In a team of five, we created a simple video game using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript over the course of a week. All design assets, sound effects, and music were made by me and the other members. We are looking to possibly expand its content in the future!
